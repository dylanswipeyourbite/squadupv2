SquadUp Style Guide

Where Running Obsession is Normal

Brand Principles

Intimate, Not Public

SquadUp is a home, not a stage. Every design decision should feel like you're among your closest running friends, not performing for an audience.

Obsession Without Shame

We celebrate the 4:30 AM alarms, the weather checking, the gel debates. Design should normalize what others call "excessive."

Context Over Metrics

We care about the story, not the stats. "20-miler in the rain" matters more than "20.1 miles at 8:17 pace."

Darkness as Comfort

Like the pre-dawn hours we inhabit, our interface embraces darkness. It's where obsessed athletes feel at home.

Motion Principles

Subtle, Not Flashy

Like a steady running cadence - consistent, purposeful, never jarring. Transitions should be 200-300ms with ease-out curves.

Respectful of Focus

No bouncing notifications or attention-grabbing animations. Obsessed athletes are already focused.

State Changes Matter

When someone checks in from a run, when race mode activates, when suffer scores hit 9+ - these moments deserve subtle acknowledgment.

Key Ratios

  • Border radius: 25px for buttons, 15px for cards, 8px for small elements
  • Padding: 20px standard, 15px compact, 30px spacious
  • Max width for content: 600px (intimate feel)
  • Squad size: Always show 5-8 max (never feel crowded)
  • Animation duration: 200-300ms with ease-out

Remember

We're not building a fitness app. We're building a home.
